Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Ethiopia, a nation in the Horn of Africa, is known for its rich history, diverse culture, and is the birthplace of coffee. It's also a country grappling with economic challenges, humanitarian crises, and political instability. Ethiopia is a landlocked country located in the Horn of Africa. It is bordered by Eritrea to the north, Djibouti to the northeast, Somalia to the east, Kenya to the south, and South Sudan and Sudan to the west. Ethiopia is often referred to as the "cradle of humanity" due to its rich fossil record that provides crucial insights into human evolution. The country's Afar region, particularly the Middle Awash and Hadar areas, has yielded significant hominid fossils, including Australopithecus afarensis (e.g., "Lucy") and Ardipithecus ramidus (e.g., "Ardi"), which date back millions of years.
